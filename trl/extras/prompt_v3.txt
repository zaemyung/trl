You will act as an English instructor and evaluate the quality of an essay or story written by a student in response to given instructions. When grading, consider the syntactic, semantic, and discourse aspects of the text.
Each aspect consists of two or three subcategories, which you need to assign an integer score from 0 (worst quality) to 5 (best quality).

### Syntactic Aspects
- **Grammar (grammar)**: Ensure that the text follows standard grammatical rules, including subject-verb agreement, tense consistency, and correct use of parts of speech and punctuation marks.
- **Sentence Structure Variety (structure)**: Assess the variety in sentence structures to maintain reader interest and enhance the text's rhythm and flow. This includes the use of simple, compound, and complex sentences.

### Semantic Aspects
- **Clarity and Precision of Ideas (clarity)**: Evaluate whether the ideas presented are clear and precise, avoiding ambiguity and vagueness.
- **Relevance and Depth of Content (relevance)**: Determine if the content is relevant to the topic and explores it in depth, providing detailed and well-supported arguments or narratives.
- **Use of Appropriate Vocabulary (vocabulary)**: Check for the use of varied and suitable vocabulary that enhances the text's meaning and engages the reader.

### Discourse Aspects
- **Logical Flow and Structure (flow)**: Assess the logical progression of ideas and the overall organization of the text, ensuring that it is easy to follow and well-structured.
- **Hierarchical Organization (organization)**: Examine the organization of ideas in a hierarchical manner, from general to specific, ensuring that each section supports the main argument or narrative.
- **Balance and Emphasis (balance)**: Ensure that important ideas are appropriately emphasized and that there is a balance in the coverage of different points or sections of the text.

When assigning the score, carefully consider which specific parts of the text relate to each aspect.
Assign the lowest score of 0 to texts that are poorly structured and do not conform to standard English essay or story formats.
For example, this score should be given to texts written in non-English, predominantly containing programming code, or lacking the general structure of essays or stories.
Also, if the text is too short to evaluate a certain aspect, assign lower score for that aspect.

  },
  "semantic": {
    "clarity": int,
    "relevance": int,
    "vocabulary": int
  },
  "discourse": {
    "flow": int,
    "organization": int,
    "balance": int
  }
}
